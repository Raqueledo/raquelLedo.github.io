Collaborations

Precious Toxicity

A mutual fascination with the mysteries of water systems and the intersection between art and science brought us together in a collaborative journey. With Robertina Šebjanič and Saša Spačal, we crafted an art&science idea, exploring how human activity has reshaped marine ecosystems. Our proposal merges scientific research with artistic exploration, using visual and interactive experiences to showcase and narrate the importance of underwater ecosystems.

Robertina Šebjanič

Robertina Šebjanič is an artist whose work investigates the biological, chemical, geopolitical, and cultural dynamics of aquatic environments, focusing on humanity's impact on other organisms. Her projects promote the need for empathetic strategies to acknowledge and respect non-human life forms..

Sasa Spačal

Saša Spačal is a postmedia artist who operates at the crossroads of living systems research, contemporary art, and sound art. Her work primarily explores the post-human condition, emphasizing that humans are just one of many components within the ecosystem rather than acting as its rulers..

SCIENTIA | CENL

As part of the Association of Spanish Scientists in the Netherlands (CENL), I am the co-coordinator of SCIENTIA, a dynamic collaboration with visual artist Ramon Trigo that brings art and science together in a shared narrative. This project bridges the gap between the two fields, highlighting their natural connection. Through exhibitions and creative installations, we bring scientific themes to life, using Ramo’s artistic approach to make complex concepts visually engaging and accessible to a wider audience. As a scientist, my role is to ensure scientific accuracy and relevance while fostering a meaningful dialogue between art and science.

Ramon Trigo

Ramon Trigo is a versatile visual artist whose self-taught practice spans painting, sculpture, illustration, and animation. His work often explores the intersection of art and science, bringing complex ideas to life through expressive, gestural brushwork and a unique blend of figurative and abstract styles. With a focus on the human figure and everyday objects, Trigo’s art has been exhibited widely and recognized with multiple awards across Spain and beyond.

Innovating Fashion for a Sustainable Future

Our collaborative project combines sustainable fashion design with scientific research on marine fungi and textile waste. We are exploring the potential of fungal strains grown under scientific conditions and integrating mycelium cellulose imagery into 3D textile sculptures. These projections will create an immersive experience, blending art, science, and sustainability. Through this project, we aim to inspire new perspectives on the future of fashion. We are seeking a space with ample white walls for projections and enough room to display three textile sculptures, ideally in a community-oriented venue that values diversity and innovation. and marine biology, aiming to create eco-friendly designs inspired by marine ecosystems and to explore how these natural elements can influence the future of sustainable fashion.

Leonie Häußermann

Leonie Häußermann is a multidisciplinary fashion designer known for her ability to create innovative ideas and products across various fields. With a background in bespoke tailoring and a degree from the University of Applied Arts in Pforzheim, her work spans fashion, illustration, and product design. Leonie has gained experience working with renowned designers such as Tim Van Steenbergen and Anne Sofie Madsen, contributing to collections and opera productions. Her design approach focuses on blending artistic expression with technical expertise, often exploring themes of sustainability and experimentation within the fashion world
